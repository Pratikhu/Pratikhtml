Chapter 8 : Information Security and Cyber Law

Information Technology

Information Technology (IT) is a modern concept or methodology of communication. It is a combination of Information and Communication Technology, which is extensively used for information flow and many other fields. IT has brought the world closer day by day, allowing us to send and receive messages across the globe in seconds. It is widely used in education, industry, banks, research centers, hospitals, and more.

In education, IT has become an essential tool for both learning and teaching, making distant learning possible. In industries, IT helps control the quantity and quality of products. It is also highly beneficial in hospitals, aiding in patient management and treatment. In entertainment, IT plays a vital role in animation and other media sectors. Additionally, scientists rely on IT for research and experiments, and space technology has become possible due to IT advancements. Thus, IT has a significant positive impact on society.

However, there are some negative aspects to IT. As its use increases, so does the prevalence of cybercrimes. Cybercriminals can steal data, expose confidential information, spread viruses, and disrupt networks, thereby affecting social peace and security.

Information Security

In a network or cyberspace, there is always the threat of cyberattacks that may lead to the loss or damage of valuable data and information. It is essential to be aware of such malicious activities and take measures to protect ourselves. Information security refers to the process of safeguarding our network infrastructure from unauthorized access, misuse, modification, destruction, or any malfunction.

We can use both physical and software preventive measures to protect ourselves from unethical activities. Information security helps create a secure environment for computers, users, and programs. It aims to mitigate security threats and attacks. A security threat is a possible danger that may cause unwanted activities, leading to a breach of security, while a security attack is an attempt to gain unauthorized access to, destroy, expose, steal, or alter valuable data and information. These attacks can be triggered by malicious applications such as viruses, spyware, Trojan horses, worms, etc.

Information security is built around three major objectives:

  • Confidentiality: Information should only be available to authorized individuals, meaning that no one can access or process another's information without proper authorization.
  • Integrity: The accuracy and completeness of data and information must be maintained, meaning unauthorized individuals cannot tamper with data.
  • Availability: Information must always be available as needed, ensuring valuable data and information can be accessed whenever required.

Cryptography

The term "cryptography" is derived from the Greek word meaning "secret writing." Cryptography is the art and science of securing information. It is primarily used to ensure secure communication between individuals, government agencies, and military forces.

Cryptography mainly involves encryption and decryption. Encryption is the process of converting plaintext (a readable message) into an unreadable format to prevent unauthorized access. The original message is transformed into unreadable text using special algorithms and keys. The unreadable text is called ciphertext. Decryption is the reverse process, converting the ciphertext back into its original, readable form.

Sometimes, encryption is also referred to as encoding or enciphering, and decryption is called decoding or deciphering.

Question and Answers

1. What challenges are we facing to create a digital society in the Nepalese context?

The problems we are facing are:Lack of digital literacy, Inadequate internet infrastructure, Cybersecurity threats, High cost of digital devices and Resistance to change.

2. What is computer ethics? What are the commandments of computer ethics?

Computer ethics refers to moral guidelines that governs the use of all computers. Commandments of Computer Ethics are as follow:
Do not use a computer to harm others.
Do not interfere with others computer work.
Do not snoop around in others files.
Do not use a computer to steal.
Do not use a computer to spread false information.

3. Discuss the role of computer ethics in the formation of a secure and reliable digital society.

It Prevents cybercrimes
Encourages responsible digital behavior
Protects privacy and data security
Promotes fairness and honesty in online interactions

4. Explain the basic principles/components of information security.

Confidentiality; Prevents unauthorized access to data.
Integrity; Ensures data accuracy and reliability.
Availability; Ensures authorized users have access to data.

5. Define cyber law. What are the major fields of cyber law?

Cyber law is a legal framework that governs the use of the internet and digital activities.
Its Major fields are: Cybercrime laws, Intellectual property laws, Data protection and privacy laws, Electronic transaction laws.

6. How do you know that your computer has been a victim of a malware attack?

Slow computer performance, Frequent crashes and system errors, Unexpected pop-ups and ads, Unauthorized changes in files, High data usage.

7. How can you protect yourself and your computer system from cybercrime?

Use strong passwords, Install antivirus software, Keep software updated, Avoid clicking on suspicious links, Enable two-factor authentication.

8. What are the advantages and disadvantages of digital signature?

Advantages are: Ensures document authenticity, Provides legal validity, Saves time and cost.
Disadvantages are: Requires internet access, Risk of hacking, Dependence on cryptographic technology.

9. Explain various areas that are generally covered in cyber law.

Cybercrimes are: Data protection, Online transactions, Intellectual property rights, Social media regulations.

10. Explain cyber law in Nepal with major provisions included.

It is Governed by the Electronic Transactions Act 2063 (2006), Defines cybercrimes and their penalties, Protects our digital transactions, Establishes rules for digital signatures.

11. List out the major objectives of ICT Policy 2072.

The major objectives are: Promote digital literacy, Enhance cybersecurity, Expand internet access, Encourage e-governance, Support IT-based industries.

Long Answer Questions

C. Long Answer Questions

1. Explain at least ten activities that are considered as cybercrime.

Hacking: Gaining unauthorized access to a computer system or network.
Identity Theft: Stealing someone's personal information to commit fraud.
Phishing: Sending fraudulent emails or messages to trick users into providing sensitive information.
Cyberstalking: Harassing, threatening, or tracking someone online.
Data Theft: Stealing or leaking confidential data.
Online Fraud: Scamming people to steal money or personal information.
Ransomware Attacks: Infecting a system with malware that locks files and demands a ransom to unlock them.
Spreading Malware: Distributing malicious software like viruses, worms, or spyware.
Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Attacks: Overloading a website or network with massive traffic to make it unavailable.
Child Exploitation and Cyber Pornography: Creating, sharing, or possessing illegal content involving minors.

2. What do you mean by malicious software? Explain any ten malicious software attacks.

Malicious software (malware) is a program designed to harm or exploit digital systems. Examples of Attacks: Virus: Infects files and spreads across systems. Trojan Horse: Disguised as legitimate software to steal data. Ransomware: Encrypts files and demands payment for decryption. Spyware: Secretly collects user information. Adware: Displays unwanted ads and tracks user activity. Keyloggers: Records keystrokes to steal passwords. Worms: Self-replicating malware that spreads across networks. Rootkits: Hides malicious processes from the operating system. Botnets: Network of infected devices controlled by hackers. Logic Bombs: Malicious code triggered by specific conditions.

3. Explain intellectual property along with its types in detail. What legal provisions do we have in Nepal to preserve intellectual property rights?

Intellectual property (IP) refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary works, and symbols. Types of Intellectual Property:
Copyright: Protects books, music, and software.
Patent: Grants rights for new inventions.
Trademark: Protects brand names and logos.
Trade secrets: Protects confidential business information.
Legal Provisions in Nepal:
Copyright Act 2059 – Protects creative works.
Patent, Design, and Trademark Act 2022 – Safeguards inventions and brands.

4. Explain digital signature along with its working mechanism.

A digital signature is an encrypted electronic signature that verifies the authenticity of digital documents. Working Mechanism:
Uses cryptographic algorithms.
A unique private key generates the signature.
The recipient verifies it using the sender’s public key.
Ensures document integrity and authentication.

5. Explain any ten policies included in ICT Policy 2072.

E-Governance Policy: Encourages digital services in government.
Cybersecurity Policy: Protects digital infrastructure.
ICT Infrastructure Development: Expands internet and technology access.
Digital Literacy Programs: Promotes ICT education.
E-Commerce Regulation: Establishes rules for online businesses.
Data Protection Law: Ensures privacy of digital data.
ICT Industry Promotion: Encourages IT-based industries.
Broadband Expansion Policy: Improves internet accessibility.
Innovation and Research Support: Encourages technological advancements.
Open Data Policy: Facilitates transparency in government information.
